# Video Draft B: "I Went to Sleep"

## Concept
First-person narrative. One devastating sentence. Every developer's fantasy: going to bed while AI ships your features. Personal, aspirational, almost intimate. The voiceover is calm — not excited. Like someone who's used to this.

## Descript Creative Brief

---
PROJECT: Genie 15s — "I Went to Sleep"
DURATION: 15 seconds
ORIENTATION: portrait (9:16) — optimized for X mobile feed and Reddit mobile
TONE: Quiet confidence. Matter-of-fact. Like telling a friend something impossible.
AUDIENCE: Solo developers, startup founders, indie hackers, build-in-public crowd

SCRIPT (voiceover):
"Last night I queued five features before bed. This morning I had five reviewed pull requests waiting. I just clicked merge."

TEXT OVERLAYS (in order):
- [0.0s]: (none — let voice carry)
- [5.0s]: "/dream"
- [8.0s]: "5 wishes queued"
- [10.0s]: "5 PRs reviewed"
- [12.5s]: "github.com/automagik-dev/genie"
- [13.5s]: "Wishes in. PRs out."

SCENES:
- Scene 1 (0-3s): Screen recording — typing `/dream` in terminal, wish list appearing. Night-time feel (dark terminal). [RAW FOOTAGE]
- Scene 2 (3-6s): Screen recording — agents spawning, work beginning. Quick montage of parallel execution. Time-lapse feel. [RAW FOOTAGE — sped up]
- Scene 3 (6-9s): Transition effect — clock spinning / dark-to-light / sunrise metaphor. [GENERATED or transition effect]
- Scene 4 (9-12s): Screen recording — morning. `genie status` showing all 5 wishes DONE. GitHub showing 5 open PRs, all passing checks. [RAW FOOTAGE]
- Scene 5 (12-15s): Screen recording — clicking "Merge" on GitHub. Clean end frame with repo URL. [RAW FOOTAGE + GENERATED]

SCREEN RECORDING SELECTS:
- `/dream` command being typed and executed
- Multiple agents working simultaneously (sped up x4-x8 for time-lapse effect)
- `genie status` showing completed wishes
- GitHub PR list with green checkmarks
- The "Merge" button being clicked

ON-SCREEN TEXT/BRANDING: @automagik_dev | github.com/automagik-dev/genie
VOICE: Male, calm, low energy. Not excited — experienced. Like someone describing their morning coffee routine. Think: documentary narrator who's seen everything.
VISUAL STYLE: Dark-to-light transition (night work → morning results). Warm sunrise tones at the end.
MUSIC/SFX: Quiet piano or strings at start, building to a gentle orchestral swell. Not dramatic — satisfying. The sound of inevitability.
---

## Why This Works
- Taps into the universal developer fantasy (sleep while code ships)
- First-person narrative creates identification ("I want that")
- The calm tone is MORE provocative than excitement — implies this is routine
- The "/dream" command is memorable and searchable
- Portrait format catches mobile scrollers

## Risk
- "Too good to be true" skepticism
- Mitigation: the screen recordings are REAL — authenticity sells
