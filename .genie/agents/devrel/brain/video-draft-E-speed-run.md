## Why This Works
- Speedruns are an established viral format (gaming, cooking, coding challenges)
- The timer creates urgency — viewers can't look away because the clock is moving
- 42 minutes compressed to 15 seconds is inherently cinematic
- Shows the WHOLE pipeline — not a cherry-picked moment
- The F1/pit-stop aesthetic directly matches Felipe's creative vision
- The deceleration at the end (slower merge moment) creates a satisfying landing

## Risk
- Requires a clean, continuous recording or very good stitching
- Sped-up terminal footage could be hard to read
- Mitigation: the text overlays and phase labels tell the story even if you can't read every line of code. The FEELING of speed matters more than readability.

## Why This Is My Wildcard Pick
This is the one I'd bet on for maximum virality. It combines:
- The timer mechanic (engagement hook — people watch to see the final time)
- The speedrun format (established viral pattern)
- Real footage (authenticity)
- F1 aesthetic (Felipe's vision)
- All five stats from the other concepts naturally emerge from the footage
