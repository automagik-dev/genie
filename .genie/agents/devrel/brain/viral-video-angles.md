# Viral Video Angles — Ranked by Impact Potential

## Angle A: The Numbers (Shock Value)
- Hook: "48 PRs. 7 days. One human."
- Why it works: Developers understand velocity. These numbers are absurd. They'll do the math.
- Risk: Could feel like vanity metrics without context.
- Best for: X/Twitter (data-driven dev audience)

## Angle B: Overnight Mode (Aspirational)
- Hook: "I went to sleep. I woke up to 5 reviewed PRs."
- Why it works: Every developer's dream. Personal, relatable, feels like a flex.
- Risk: Sounds too good to be true without proof.
- Best for: Reddit (skeptical audience wants to believe)

## Angle C: Context Collapse (Visual Story)
- Hook: 7 chaotic terminals → orchestrated harmony
- Why it works: Visual storytelling. The problem IS the video. Music does the emotional work.
- Risk: Needs strong screen recordings. More production effort.
- Best for: LinkedIn (professional narrative), X (visual scroll-stop)

## Angle D: Self-Hosting (Meta Proof)
- Hook: "This repo ships itself. 96% of its own PRs pass on first review."
- Why it works: Self-referential proof. Genie builds Genie. That's inherently viral.
- Risk: Inside-baseball for non-technical audiences.
- Best for: X, Reddit (dev communities love meta)

## Angle E: The Speed Run
- Hook: "Idea to merged PR in 42 minutes. No human wrote the code."
- Why it works: Time-lapse energy. Speed is inherently cinematic.
- Risk: Might trigger "AI replacing jobs" backlash.
- Best for: All platforms (universal appeal of speed)

## Production Notes
- All angles work with: orchestral music + fast cuts + minimal text
- Screen recordings of real agent sessions are the hero content
- The "Shell F1 90s ads" aesthetic works best with Angles C and E
- Angles A and D work best with bold typography, minimal visuals
